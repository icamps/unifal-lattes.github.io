Marcelo Lacerda Rezende

Graduado em Engenharia Agronômica (1994) e mestre em Economia Rural pela Universidade Federal de Viçosa (1998). Doutor em Economia Aplicada pela Escola Superior de Agricultura Luiz de Queiróz - Universidade de São Paulo (2001). Pós-doutorado no Department of Agricultural Economics da Texas AM University - EUA (2013). Atualmente é professor titular da Universidade Federal de Alfenas e membro permanente do programa de mestrado em Economia. Tem experiência na área de Economia, com ênfase em Economia Agrária, atuando principalmente nos seguintes temas: geração de renda, desenvolvimento local-regional e estudos de viabilidade econômica e risco.                                       3. 2013-2013. Economic feasibility of sugar and ethanol production in Brazil under alternative future prices outlook
                                        Descrição: The objective of this work is to project the viability of the industry under alternative future price outlooks (2013-2022). A Monte Carlo simulation model was developed to simulate a representative sugar mill in São Paulo state, Brazil. The economic feasibility study was conducted using four different price scenarios forecasted by FAPRI-ISU, OECD-FAO, USDA and World Bank. The model starts witch year with a stochastic annual sugarcane yield and area harvested, defined by user. Monthly production is simulated the monthly production for white sugar, VHP sugar, anhydrous and hydrated ethanol based on stochastic monthly sugar cane production. In the next section, the cost section, total cost is simulated for cane production and production of each output. The third section starts with the annual total cost and annual total receipts to simulate net cash income and calculates annual cash flows. The last section in the model simulates the balance sheet and calculates net present value (NPV) and rate of return on equity (ROE). The results showed a high probability of success for OECD-FAO, USDA and World Bank scenarios where the NPV and ROE did not show values less than zero. The FAPRI price scenario presented a small probability of negatives values for NPV and ROE, and indicated the largest relative risk for these two variables. These results help to better understand the outlook for the Brazilian sugar and ethanol industry over the next 10 years. The Brazilian sugar and ethanol industry will continue to experience a high degree of risk and uncertainty from production, macroeconomics variables, demand, costs of production and market price. As a result, the industry will likely see considerable risk in rates of return, net cash income, and ending cash; however, there is a high probability the industry will be profitable..
